Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Values

I did a bit of research for questions to ask myself and topics to consider, in order to find out what my values are. This is a difficult topic for me, because while there are immediate things that come to mind, I have always felt that in a lot of ways I don’t really know who I am and that I’m still figuring it out - I’m constantly learning new things about myself. Probably the last time I solidly reflected on my values was in my final years at high school for some homework task. This is what I came up with as my core personal values, and why:

Connection - I value the times I’m able to feel a connection with myself. Above all though, I value the connection with people I love, friends, and even strangers as being a vital part of my life and making me who I am today. I consider myself a bit of an introverted extrovert; I need to get out and see my friends and socialise, but at the same time without some solid time alone to be entirely by myself I cannot recharge either. In my mid this comes back to connection, the connection to self and others. The connection I have and feel with people 100% influences how I feel in life, and brings me a great happiness. The best memories I have, the most immediate ones that pop to mind are all to do with spending quality time with the people important to me and building a deep bond by talking and sharing in honesty. I think I feel this way because to me connection is what makes us human.

Honesty - Without honesty I wouldn’t have the best relationships that I have made in my life. I wouldn’t have grown as a person and learnt about others that matter to me. I think honestly is key, we need to understand our strengths as well as our shortcomings. It can be tough to be honest, especially when it hurts our ego or pride, but that is especially why I value it. I also think it is the most important quality in our connections with each other in creating bonds, and is the foundation of respect.

Experiences - This sounds a little vague at first, but to clarify I’m talking about experiencing things outside of our norm. For instance, moving out from home or to a foreign country, creating something, failing at something. Experience is the cornerstone of what makes us all unique; all our experiences are different even if they can be similar at times, and this is why it is so important to me. My experiences have pushed me outside of my comfort zone and posed my boundaries in for both the better and worst. Ultimately it is how I think we learn about ourselves and to open our eyes to what is around us.

Work ethic / Effort - In my mind and experience there is no substitute for work ethic and actually putting in the effort. I have seen people turn their life around and build something out of nothing through simply hard work and effort. It is something that can be developed, but this also ironically requires the effort to do so. This is something that I am constantly working towards developing in myself.

Acceptance - For the longest time I never really felt accepted, and I think in part a large part of this was also not knowing myself all that well too and I didn’t have the confidence to be myself. This is not necessarily that people weren’t accepting but rather that I might not have been aware. I think this is why it is important to me. I remember vividly the realisation and the feeling and happiness of being accepted by a friend. This is what makes us feel free to be individual, and is where I think our greatest strengths. Being an accepting person as well as being accepted means we are able to embrace the differences and share a connection. I try to encourage this attitude in myself and to see it reflected in others.

Individuality - This ties in with acceptance. Having the confidence to be individual and not change yourself to suit other people is something that I have always struggled with. People who are able to do that are the ones I really admire. I know we all have our own self-doubts, but I often find myself very self critical. I have learned that we can’t change everyone and that at times you just have to be yourself regardless of whether people will accept it or not.

Creativity - I think creativity is the manifestation of our individuality. They are so linked that without one you almost couldn't have the other. It offers a way for us to express our individuality and that is why I value it. There can never be a right or wrong when it comes to creativity, it just is.

I arrived at these values by asking myself about times that have made me the happiest, times I felt satisfied in life or fulfilled, moment I felt the most proud, and lastly the people I love and admire most. I asked myself other questions along with the above, such as who was there, what other factors influenced the feeling and so.

Identity

I think my very European family has likely influence my values and identity more than I realised when I was young. Everyone is very family orientated, and my grandparents are central figures in the family. They came from growing up in WWII with nothing, and then moved to NZ with nothing and built their lives from scratch. I this has made an intrenched attitude of appreciation and a lack of regard for wealth as being important. Overall probably a hard work ethic and determination are common values in the family. Growing up amongst this, being very close with my grandparents and seeing the people around me act in this way undoubtedly influenced the was I act and think. I think also the fact that we eat certain Dutch foods, speak some words and frequently have relatives over to NZ to visit has also instilled a feeling or being dutch, although I’ve never actually been there and hardly speak the language.

Strengths

I think my main strengths are my work ethic, determination and awareness. I have a deep motivation to accomplish what I set my mind to and this drives me through doing things and completing them right until the end. I don’t like to leave things unfinished. I also have a keen awareness of others and how they feel, where they struggle, and I think this allows me to work well with anybody - I can honestly say I don’t think I’ve every been in a situation where work or study has been tough because of interpersonal tensions. However I am always very self critical, and this can set me back at times and I doubt myself and struggle to find the motivation or drive if I get in my head. Also because of this I struggle to take any form of praise or complement, but on the flip side tend to value constructive criticism more. For some reason in my head it feel more practical, and I tend to have my doubts that positive feedback is genuine at times. I think my strength will definitely be an asset on this learning journey as learning anything new require a lot of effort and hardwork.

The only instance I can think of where there was a tension, was in my last job. I had been working there for 3 years along with my colleague. We were working on a project to retrofit some equipment, and the method he proposed seemed totally illogical to me. I politely offered my method to the group as an alternative, at which point my colleague insisted that his was the right way to go and that was that, end of discussion. I handled this by simply removing myself from the situation because I was aware that there was something going on here with him where he felt he needed it done his way. Maybe he felt he had something to prove, or disagreed with my suggestion, which is fine either way, but I recognised that then and there was not the time to discuss it. I waited a day or so for the tension to die down, before trying to discuss this again. At this time things were more calm and we had a discussion coming to a compromise. I think this is the nature of people, we all have lives and who knows what is influencing or affecting someone at any given time, but recognising this and having awareness of others helps.
